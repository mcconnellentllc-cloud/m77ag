Step-by-Step Guide

How to Submit Your Farmer Bridge Assistance Application

Enrollment is open now. Follow these 5 steps to review and submit your pre-filled FBA application online through farmers.gov.

Enrollment Open: Feb 23 – Apr 17, 2026

Before You Start

  • You must have a Login.gov account already set up and linked to farmers.gov
  • Your 2025 acreage report must have been filed by Dec 19, 2025
  • Your identity must be verified — if you haven't done this, call your FSA office first
  • Use Chrome, Edge, or Firefox — Safari does not work with farmers.gov
Haven't set up Login.gov yet? Stop here. Go to youraginfo.com and follow our Login.gov setup guide first. You can't submit your application without it.
1
Log In to Farmers.gov

Go to farmers.gov and log in with your Login.gov credentials.

Once you're logged in, you'll see the welcome page. Right at the top there's a green banner for the FSA Farmer Bridge Assistance (FBA) Program.

Click the Apply button under the FBA Program banner.
2
Go to FBA Applications

You'll see a welcome message for the Farmer Bridge Assistance Program application. It shows your name and tells you to search for all applications you have authority to certify.

Click the green Go to FBA applications → button.
Be patient. Loading your applications may take up to 30 seconds. Do not refresh the page.
3
Select Your Application

You'll see a list of all your applications — one for each individual or business entity you have authority to certify. Each one will show a status of "Not Submitted" in orange.

Select the application you want to submit by clicking on it (it will highlight with a green border).

Click on the application, then click Continue with selected application →
Multiple entities? If you farm under multiple entities (personal, LLC, partnership, etc.), you'll see a separate application for each one. You'll need to submit each application individually. Come back to this screen and repeat for each entity.
Don't see an application? If you believe you should have one and it's not listed, contact your local FSA county office immediately. Your acreage report may not have been filed by the Dec 19 deadline, or there may be a record issue.
4
Review, Certify, and Submit

The application is pre-filled with your information based on your 2025 acreage report. Review everything carefully. Make sure your acres, crops, and shares are correct.

Scroll down to the "Certify and submit" section at the bottom.

You'll see a citizenship certification statement. Select "Yes" to confirm you are a U.S. citizen or resident alien (or that your entity is organized under State Law with all members being citizens or resident aliens).

Then check the box to place your electronic signature on the application.

Click Submit Application
This is a legal certification. You are signing under penalty of perjury that the information is true and correct. Make sure you've reviewed everything before submitting. You can also click "Download Application Form" in the top right to save a copy for your records.
After you submit, you will receive a green confirmation banner at the top of the screen confirming your submission was successful. If you don't see that banner, your application did not go through — try again.
Pro Tip — Submit button greyed out? If you have multiple entities and the Submit button at the bottom is light blue and won't let you click it, open all of the dropdown sections on the application to review the documents. This will trigger a data refresh. Once the refresh completes, the Submit button will turn active and you can submit.
5
Confirm Submission

After submitting, you'll be taken back to the applications list. Your application status will now show "Submitted" in green.

If you have multiple entities, repeat Steps 3 and 4 for each one until all applications show "Submitted."

All applications should show Submitted in green. You're done.

Your payment will be deposited into the bank account FSA has on file. If you need to update your banking info, contact your local FSA office before payments go out.
