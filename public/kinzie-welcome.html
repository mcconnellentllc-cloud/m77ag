How to Pay Rent

Rent is due on the 1st of each month. Colorado law gives you a 7-day grace period before a late fee applies.

Log in to the portal, go to Payments → Set up ACH, and connect your bank account. You can pay one-off each month or turn on autopay for the 1st. Autopay earns you a $25/month rent reduction as long as it's active. Contact Kyle to receive routing/account info for direct ACH.

Rent Reduction Options You Elected

The lease you signed allows you to earn credits against the $1,400 base rent by taking on specific responsibilities. Whatever you've checked on your signed lease is what's active on your account. If you'd like to change your selections mid-lease, contact Kyle in writing.

Reminder: each rent reduction is tied to a real responsibility. If we end up doing the task (mowing, snow removal, minor repair, etc.), we can revoke that reduction going forward and back-charge the reasonable cost. We'll always give you written notice before doing so, and we'd rather work it out with you than revoke anything.

Improvement Projects — Earn Up to $150/Month

If you want to do a paint job, install flooring, tackle exterior maintenance, or a similar improvement, we'll consider it. Here's how it works:

  1. Send us a written proposal via the portal Messages tab (or email office@m77ag.com). Include what you want to do, expected materials, and roughly how long it'll take.
  2. We approve in writing before you start. No approval, no credit — please don't start the work or buy materials on your own.
  3. We buy the materials once we approve. You supply the labor.
  4. We inspect when you're done. If it's up to standard, we apply the credit (up to $150) to the next month's rent.

Utilities

We pay all utilities — electricity, propane, water, sewer, and trash. That's a real benefit and a real cost. Historical monthly averages are about $150 for electricity year-round and $250 for propane during heating season (Nov–Mar). If your usage runs more than 20% above those averages in a given month, we'll reach out and talk about it — we'd rather adjust together than surprise you.
