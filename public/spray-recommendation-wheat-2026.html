4. Tank-Mix Order (W-A-L-E-S)

Fill spray tank to ⅓ full with clean water and start agitation. Add products in this order, allowing each to disperse fully before adding the next:

  1. W — Water (½ tank, agitator running).
  2. A — Hydrovant (adjuvant / water conditioner — added first so it can precondition the water).
  3. L — Flumi (dry/liquid formulation — add before EC herbicides).
  4. E — Dicamba, then 2,4-D LV6 (emulsifiable concentrates).
  5. S — Glyphosate (soluble liquid — added last).
  6. Top off with the remaining water. Maintain agitation until spray-out.

5. Application & Safety Notes

Dicamba Buffer Requirement. Do not apply within the label-required downwind buffer of dicamba-sensitive crops (soybeans, tomatoes, grapes, orchards). Consult the current dicamba label for the specific buffer distance and wind-speed restrictions in effect.
  1. Wind: 3–10 mph. Do not apply during temperature inversions (typical dawn/dusk when smoke or dust hangs level in the air).
  2. Temperature: Above 90°F increases volatility risk for dicamba and 2,4-D — schedule for cooler parts of the day.
  3. Nozzle / droplet size: Use a labeled low-drift nozzle (e.g., TTI or ULD) at coarse-to-extremely-coarse droplet spectrum. Boom pressure per nozzle chart.
  4. Carrier volume: Minimum 10 gal/ac by ground; 15 gal/ac preferred for full residual activation of flumi.
  5. PPE: Long sleeves/pants, chemical-resistant gloves, protective eyewear during mixing/loading. Refer to individual product labels for full PPE.
  6. REI / PHI: Follow the most restrictive re-entry interval among products in the mix.
  7. Recordkeeping: Record application date, wind, temperature, rate, and applicator initials for each field in the spray log.
  8. Cleanout: Triple-rinse tank and nozzles before switching to any sensitive crop (soybeans, alfalfa, sunflowers). Use ammonia-based tank cleaner after dicamba.
